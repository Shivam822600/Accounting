Frequently Asked Questions

I'm an academic at La Trobe University in Bundoora — what can I claim?
Academic staff can claim: books and subscriptions relevant to your research, conference attendance where you’re presenting or it’s directly related to your role, home office costs for regular at-home work, and computer equipment used for work. The ATO applies the ‘wholly, exclusively and necessarily’ test. We’ll work through your specific role and what qualifies.
I'm a PhD student with a research stipend and some tutoring income. Do I need to lodge?
Research Training Program stipends are generally tax-exempt. However, if you have additional income from tutoring, casual academic work, or external consulting, you may need to lodge. The interaction between exempt and taxable income is a common area of confusion. A brief conversation with us usually resolves it quickly.
I'm an allied health practitioner in Bundoora — is my business structure still tax-efficient?
Allied health practitioners often start as sole traders and later question whether a company or service trust structure would save them money. The answer depends on gross income, liability considerations and long-term plans. We’ll model the numbers and give you a grounded recommendation.
Does RPS handle SMSF for Bundoora professionals?
Yes. SMSF is a growing part of our practice, particularly for professionals who want more control over their retirement savings. We handle setup, annual compliance, auditing, and contribution strategy advice.
