FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers on appointments, what to bring, refund timing, and catching up on late returns. Don’t see your question? Call us — we’ll answer it on the spot

Call 1300 110 120
Do I need to come into the office, or can I do it all online?

Either works. About half our clients lodge fully online through our secure portal, phone and email. The other half prefer face-to-face — especially first-timers, sole traders with cash income, and clients with rental properties or overseas income to sort through.

What do I need to bring to my tax appointment?

For a standard return, bring photo ID, your bank account details for the refund, and any records for the deductions you want to claim — work-from-home logs, motor vehicle logbook, uniform receipts, self-education, donations. We pull your PAYG income statement, interest, dividends and private health details directly from the ATO once you authorise us, so you don’t need to chase those down. After the initial consult we’ll send you a tailored checklist based on your situation.

How long does it take to get my tax refund?

Once we lodge electronically, the ATO typically processes refunds within 7–14 business days and deposits straight into your nominated bank account. Returns with rental income, capital gains, or first-time lodgements can take a little longer, especially if the ATO flags them for review. We track every return after lodgement and let you know if anything needs follow-up.

I haven't lodged a tax return for a few years — can you help?

Yes  we regularly catch up clients who are 2 to 10+ years behind. We’ll work out which years are outstanding, lodge them in the right order, and where penalties or interest have built up, we can help negotiate with the ATO and set up a payment plan if needed. The longer it sits, the worse it gets so the sooner we start, the better the outcome.
